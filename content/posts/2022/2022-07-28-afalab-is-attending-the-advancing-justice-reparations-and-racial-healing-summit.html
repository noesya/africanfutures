AfaLab will be speaking on a panel at the Advancing Justice: Reparations and Racial Healing Summit in Accra, Ghana from August 1st to 4th, 2022. This summit intends to respond to the uneven responses currently being spurred on the question of reparations for the legacies of the Transatlantic slave trade and colonialism in Africa and the diaspora. It will serve as an opportunity to leverage existing initiatives, amplify that work, and illuminate opportunities to harness various interests and aspirations.


The specific aims following the end of the Summit:

  • To develop deeper, sophisticated, and more nuanced campaign strategies and the capacity to collectively advocate for reparatory justice and facilitate cross-country learning from contexts that have successfully pursued reparatory justice for historical crimes and injustices.

  • To develop an agreement on what could be included in a continental framework/strategy that would guide the process of achieving reparatory justice for historical injustices.

  • To secure commitment from influential European players to push for process of reparatory justice from governments, financial institutions, and corporations in response to demands from CARICOM and emerging African Reparation Commissions 

  • Developing a preliminary basis of materials that will inform a policy and advocacy agendas across continents as well as support and amplify existing policy advocacy agendas 

  • To foster intercontinental reflections and collaborations with key actors across the continents. To globalize the discourse on reparatory justice and healing vii. To offer an avenue for showcasing creativity on socio-communal healing methods and approaches by Africans and People of African descent. 

  • To curate and collate the myriad of initiatives and formulate a comprehensive advocacy agenda for reparative justice and racial healing for historical crimes.


The summit will be co-hosted by the African Union Commission (AUC), the Africa Transitional Justice Legacy Fund (ATJLF), The Africa-America Institute (AAI), and Global Black. The event is sponsored by the MacArthur Foundation.

The European launch of the AfaLab will be happening at the 'What's to be repaired? - Postcolonial Justice in Postcolonial Europe.'  The Co-Directors, Amah Edoh and Liliane Umubyeyi, will present their strategy for addressing historic and ongoing racial injustices resulting from the legacies of colonialism and slavery.

When: 23rd of June from 16:00 to 17:30.

Location (in-person and online available): Casco Art Institue  - Lange Nieuwstraat 7,  3512PA Utrecht. 

Access online through this site. Click the "Thursday" link at 4pm local time to join.

This event will be taking place during the international symposium at Utrecht University on June 23rd. Co-Directors Amah Edoh and Liliane Umubyeyi will speak on the need to build a reparative justice agenda in Europe. 

The symposium will also host Mame-Fatou Niang, an AfaLab Fellow. As part of the Lecture series Race et culture, she will be presenting "French, but not (Q) White?: Expanding Frenchness for the 21st Century."

For the daily programme, locations, and further updates, please check the event website. 

For registration, please send an email to this address: whatstoberepaired2022@gmail.com

Founded by Liliane Umubyeyi and Amah Edoh in the aftermath of the 2020 global Black Lives Matter protests, the African Futures Lab challenges the lack of accountability for racial injustices across Europe & Africa.Â

The racial justice movements of 2020 highlighted the fact that racial injustice is a global and structural concern that is neither limited to national contexts, nor to individual prejudice. They underscored the importance of conceptualizing race and racism as a system of global dominance.


However, responses to the protests from former colonial powers largely ignored the structural and global aspects of racial injustice, reducing racial inequalities to a matter of individual prejudice confined to national borders.


We established the Lab to challenge this enduring denial of the global and structural manifestations of racial inequalities with evidence-based advocacy and policy recommendations.

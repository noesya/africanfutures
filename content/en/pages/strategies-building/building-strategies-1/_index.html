The invisibility of global racial injustices has made it difficult for civil society actors and private or public entities to advocate for justice and reparations or to take responsibility.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

A lot of expertise is needed to prove the responsibility of the authors of these global racial injustices. AfaLab supports civil society organizations, as well as private and public entities, in building strategies to tackle these global racial injustices.

This support is built through collaborative working spaces (Mobilization Labs or MobLabs) where strategies can be developed and/or strengthened.

As multiple forms of expertise are necessary for revealing the many facets of global racial injustices, MobLabs bring together a range of specialists: historians, economists, anthropologists, lawyers, community organizers, communication experts, and others relevant experts. 

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: alt_background text: >-

MobLabs last from a few weeks to several months, depending on the project, and target our program areas: Environmental Violence, Gender Violence, Socio-economic Violence, and Cultural Heritage.
